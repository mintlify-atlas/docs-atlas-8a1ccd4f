# Moderation overview

The Urbanshade OS moderation panel is the central hub for admins to manage users, review reports, monitor NAVI activity, and keep the platform running smoothly.

<Warning>
  This documentation is for authorized administrators only. The moderation panel contains sensitive tools that can permanently affect user accounts. Handle with care.
</Warning>

## Who moderators are

Moderation in Urbanshade OS is handled by a small team of trusted, appointed staff. There are no public applications — all staff positions are granted directly by the Creator.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Creator (Aswd)" icon="crown">
    Full control over everything. Can grant and revoke admin status, assign VIP status, and access owner-only features such as lockdowns. The only person who can make someone an admin.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Administrator" icon="shield">
    Full access to the moderation panel, user management, bans, warnings, and most system controls. Cannot grant admin status to others or access owner-only features.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Trial Admins have access to a limited subset of moderation tools while in their probationary period. They operate under the supervision of full Admins.
</Note>

## Moderation scope

The moderation panel covers the following areas:

- **User management** — Search, view, and manage any user account. Review a user's moderation history, warnings, and ban status.
- **Moderation actions** — Issue warnings, apply temporary or permanent bans, grant VIP status, and manage admin roles.
- **NAVI monitoring** — Real-time visibility into automated activity, message filtering, lockout events, and autonomous response settings.
- **Reports** — Review and resolve reports submitted by users.
- **Statistics** — Analytics dashboard with user growth, moderation metrics, role distribution, and activity patterns.
- **Quick actions** — System-wide controls including site lockdown, global broadcasts, and maintenance mode.

## Accessing the panel

The moderation panel is available at `/moderation`.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to an admin account">
    Make sure you are signed in with an account that has the Admin or Creator role. Your badge will be visible in the UI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to /moderation">
    Go to `/moderation` directly, or use the admin link in your profile menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your access level">
    If you see a **Demo Mode** banner at the top, your account does not have admin access. All actions in demo mode are simulated and do not affect real users.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Non-admins can visit `/moderation` to explore the interface in demo mode. This is a great way to understand the tools before needing to use them.
</Note>

## Responsibilities

With access to the moderation panel comes responsibility. All admins are expected to:

- Apply rules **consistently** across all users, regardless of personal relationships
- Always include a **specific reason** when taking any action
- Follow the **progressive discipline** model — warnings before bans, temporary before permanent
- Be prepared to **justify decisions** in the event of an appeal
- **Never abuse** moderation powers for personal gain or to settle personal disputes

<Warning>
  Every moderation action is permanently logged with a timestamp, the acting admin's identity, and the stated reason. Abuse of moderation powers will result in role revocation.
</Warning>
