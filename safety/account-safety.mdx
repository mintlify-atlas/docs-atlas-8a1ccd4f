# Account safety

Your Urbanshade OS account is your gateway to the facility. Keeping it secure protects your settings, cloud-synced data, and your reputation in the community.

<Warning>
  No one from Urbanshade OS — including Aswd and all admins — will **ever** ask for your password. If someone asks for it, they are attempting to steal your account. Report them immediately.
</Warning>

## Security best practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Strong password" icon="key">
    Use a unique password with at least 12 characters. Mix uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse a password from another site.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Two-factor authentication" icon="fingerprint">
    Enable 2FA in your account settings. Even if your password is compromised, 2FA prevents unauthorized access by requiring a second verification step.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Privacy settings" icon="eye-off">
    Review who can see your profile and activity. Control your visibility in account settings to limit exposure to unwanted contact.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Session management" icon="lock">
    Always log out on shared or public devices. Periodically review your active sessions and revoke any you do not recognize.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Password recommendations

This is a simulation environment, so the stakes are lower than a banking app — but it is still good practice to use a secure, unique password.

- Use at least 12 characters
- Avoid using your real name, username, or common words
- Never use a password you have already used on another service
- Consider using a password manager to generate and store strong passwords
- Change your password if you believe it may have been exposed

<Note>
  Urbanshade OS passwords are hashed and stored securely via Supabase. Your plaintext password is never stored.
</Note>

## Two-factor authentication and session management

**Enabling 2FA** adds an extra layer of protection. Even if an attacker gets your password, they cannot access your account without also completing the second factor. Enable 2FA in **Settings → Account → Security**.

**Session management** lets you see all devices and browsers currently logged into your account. If you see a session you do not recognize:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to account settings">
    Open **Settings → Account → Active Sessions**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review active sessions">
    Check the list of active sessions for any unfamiliar devices, locations, or browsers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revoke unknown sessions">
    Click **Revoke** next to any session you do not recognize. This immediately logs out that session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Change your password">
    After revoking unknown sessions, change your password to prevent the same attacker from logging back in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What data is stored

Urbanshade OS stores the following data when you have a cloud account:

| Data | Where it is stored | Notes |
|------|--------------------|-------|
| Username and display name | Supabase cloud database | Visible to other users |
| Email address | Supabase auth | Used for login only, not displayed |
| Avatar image | Supabase storage | Optional |
| Settings and preferences | Supabase cloud database | Synced across devices |
| Desktop layout and icons | Supabase cloud database | Part of cloud sync |
| Installed apps | Supabase cloud database | Part of cloud sync |
| Session data (no account) | Browser `localStorage` | Cleared when you clear browser data |

<Note>
  If you are not logged in, all data is stored only in your browser's `localStorage`. This data does not persist across different browsers or devices and is lost when you clear your browser storage.
</Note>

## Recognizing threats

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Phishing attempts">
    Be wary of links promising "free VIP status" or "admin access." Real staff never send external links asking you to verify your account or claim a reward. If a link looks suspicious, do not click it — report it to an admin.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Impersonation">
    Anyone pretending to be Aswd or an admin without the proper system badge is fake. Real staff always have their badge visible. Check the badge before trusting any authority claim.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Suspicious account activity">
    If you notice unfamiliar logins, settings changes you did not make, or messages you did not send, your account may have been compromised. Act immediately using the steps below.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## If your account is compromised

Act quickly. Every minute counts when an attacker has access to your account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Change your password immediately">
    Use a completely new password you have never used anywhere else. Make it long and complex.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable 2FA if not already enabled">
    This prevents future unauthorized access even if your new password is later exposed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revoke all active sessions">
    Go to **Settings → Account → Active Sessions** and revoke everything. This kicks out anyone currently logged in, including the attacker.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact an admin">
    Message a verified admin (check their badge) to let them know what happened. They can help investigate and take additional steps to secure your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>
